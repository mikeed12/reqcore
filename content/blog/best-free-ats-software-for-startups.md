# Best Free ATS Software for Startups (2026)

The best free ATS software for startups depends on what kind of "free" you can live with. Some tools give you a permanent free plan with hard limits on jobs or users. Others offer a time-limited trial of their full product. And open source ATS platforms are genuinely free with no feature restrictions — you host them yourself.

We evaluated dozens of free ATS options and narrowed the list to seven that actually work for early-stage startups hiring their first 5–50 people. This is not a list padded with enterprise tools that happen to have a demo button.

## Three Kinds of "Free" ATS — and Why It Matters

ATS vendors use the word "free" loosely. The differences between these three models determine whether your free tool becomes a permanent solution or a 14-day countdown to a sales call:

| Free Model | What You Get | What You Give Up | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Free-forever plan** | Core features, no time limit | Limited jobs (usually 1–3), restricted users, paywalled integrations | Startups with 1–2 active roles at a time |
| **Free trial** | Full feature access for 7–18 days | Time pressure, no long-term use without paying | Evaluating a paid product before committing |
| **Open source** | Full software, unlimited everything | You handle hosting, updates, and infrastructure | Technical founders who deploy with Docker |

Most roundup articles mix these categories without telling you. A 14-day trial of a $166/month product is not the same thing as a permanently free plan. We label every tool in this guide so you know exactly what you are signing up for.

For a deeper analysis of how these categories differ, read our [comparison of open source vs free ATS](/blog/open-source-vs-free-ats).

## What Startups Actually Need From an ATS

Enterprise ATS feature lists run long. Startups hiring their first team members need a shorter list done well:

- **Job posting to free boards.** Your opening needs to appear on Indeed, LinkedIn, and Google for Jobs without per-posting fees.
- **A candidate pipeline.** A Kanban-style view showing who is at which stage — applied, screened, interviewed, offered.
- **Resume storage.** One place for resumes instead of scattered email attachments and shared Drive folders.
- **Basic collaboration.** The ability to leave notes on candidates so the founder and hiring manager stay aligned.
- **Email from the platform.** Sending rejection or next-step emails without switching to Gmail.

What you do not need yet: AI candidate scoring, advanced analytics dashboards, HRIS integrations, branded career microsites, or compliance audit trails. These features are valuable at 100+ employees. At 10 employees, they are upsell opportunities disguised as necessities.

## Dover — Best Free ATS for AI-Powered Hiring

Dover is the standout in this list. It offers a genuinely free ATS with unlimited job postings, access to 75+ job boards, AI-powered candidate sorting, and automated confirmation emails. The interface is clean and startup-oriented — no enterprise complexity hiding behind layers of menus.

The AI chatbot filters candidates by criteria like years of experience and returns results in seconds. The sequencing tool auto-generates personalized outreach emails using your company information, which we found surprisingly accurate during testing.

**Limitations:** Dover does not support employee referral programs natively. Resume parsing is English-only. The platform integrates best with Google Workspace — if your startup runs on Microsoft 365, expect friction with calendar and email syncing.

**After free:** Dover's business model is a Recruiting Marketplace where you hire on-demand recruiters who set their own rates. The ATS itself remains free regardless of whether you use the marketplace.

## Wellfound — Best Free ATS for Hiring Tech Talent

Wellfound (formerly AngelList Talent) gives startups unlimited job postings and access to over 10 million job seekers who specifically want startup roles. For engineering and product positions, the talent pool quality is consistently stronger than what you find on LinkedIn or general-purpose job boards.

The in-app messaging system keeps conversations tied to candidate profiles — no external email threads to lose track of. One user described hiring 10+ people through the platform in a single year, citing that "the talent pool there is consistently more relevant to our needs than any other platform."

**Limitations:** No resume parsing, no sourcing tools, and no job board integrations beyond Wellfound's own platform. No mobile app. If you need to hire non-tech roles — operations, sales, support — the candidate pool thins out fast.

**After free:** Wellfound recently gated features like advanced search filters and saved templates behind paid tiers. The core ATS remains free, but the search experience has degraded compared to the AngelList Talent days.

## Zoho Recruit — Best Free ATS With Built-In CRM

Zoho Recruit's free plan includes candidate management, five customizable email templates, interview scheduling, and posting to both free and paid job boards. It supports 26 languages, which is useful if your startup is hiring internationally from day one.

The integration with other Zoho products (CRM, People, Books) creates a broader ecosystem play. If your startup already uses Zoho for anything, Recruit fits naturally.

**Limitations:** One active job opening at a time. One recruiter account. 256MB storage — roughly 250 resumes before you hit the ceiling. Customer support hours were recently cut from 24/5 to 8/5 for free users. One recruiter who used the free plan daily noted that "it works well for small companies with one or two recruiters," but cautioned that "customer support is not very strong."

**After free:** Paid plans start at $25/user/month billed annually. The jump from one active job to multiple jobs requires at minimum the Standard plan. The 45-day money-back guarantee reduces commitment risk.

## Loxo — Best Free ATS for Recruiting Agencies

Loxo's free plan includes unlimited jobs, a drag-and-drop pipeline, resume parsing, and CRM features that most free ATS tools skip entirely: a project Kanban board, task management, client portal, and invoicing. For agencies and staffing teams, this combination is rare at $0.

**Limitations:** One user seat on the free plan. No contact info enrichment, no outreach tools, no free job board integrations, and no HRIS connections. The Chrome extension is required for full functionality — other browsers get a limited experience. The diversity search filter had bugs in recent testing.

**After free:** Paid plans start at $169/seat/month on annual billing. That is a steep jump — the largest on this list — reflecting Loxo's positioning as a full recruitment suite rather than a lightweight startup ATS.

## MightyRecruiter — Best Free ATS for Unlimited Job Postings

MightyRecruiter offers unlimited job postings across 29+ job boards, unlimited users, access to a 22-million-candidate resume database for preview, a branded career page, and a reporting dashboard — all free. It includes 200+ job description templates and suggests candidates based on your criteria.

**Limitations:** Viewing full candidate resumes from the database requires payment (pay-per-view model). No interview scheduling, no workflow automation, no collaborative hiring features. Integration is limited to job boards and social media — no calendar or HRIS connections.

**After free:** MightyRecruiter monetizes through its resume database access. The core ATS features remain free indefinitely. This makes it one of the more sustainable free models since the business does not depend on converting you to a subscription.

## BreezyHR — Best Free ATS for Pipeline Visualization

BreezyHR's free Bootstrap plan gives you a drag-and-drop Kanban pipeline and team collaboration tools in an interface that feels more polished than most paid alternatives. The user experience is the primary reason to choose BreezyHR — recruiting teams consistently rate it as one of the easiest ATS platforms to learn.

**Limitations:** One active job position or candidate pool. That single constraint is the deal-breaker for most startups. The moment you hire for two roles simultaneously, you need a paid plan.

**After free:** The Startup plan costs $157/month and unlocks unlimited positions. Pricing is per-company rather than per-seat — a better model than per-recruiter pricing as your hiring team grows.

## When to Upgrade From a Free ATS

Free ATS tools work until they do not. Watch for these signals:

1. **You are hiring for 3+ roles simultaneously.** Most free plans cap active jobs at 1–3. Constantly rotating which role is "active" means losing candidates who applied to deactivated postings.
2. **Multiple people need access.** Zoho Recruit and Loxo limit free plans to one user. When your co-founder and a hiring manager both need the system, you need a paid plan or an open source tool with no seat limits.
3. **You cannot export your candidate data.** Free plans rarely include robust data export. If you switch tools later, your candidate history may not transfer. This is the [vendor lock-in problem](/blog/open-source-vs-free-ats) that open source avoids entirely.
4. **Compliance becomes non-optional.** GDPR consent tracking, right-to-deletion workflows, and audit trails are never on free plans. Once you process candidate data from the EU, these shift from nice-to-have to [legal requirements](/blog/self-hosted-vs-cloud-ats).
5. **Manual processes are costing you hires.** According to [SHRM research](https://www.shrm.org/topics-tools/news/talent-acquisition), the average cost-per-hire is around $4,700. When slow tooling adds weeks to your hiring cycle and you lose candidates to faster-moving competitors, the $25–$165/month for a paid ATS costs far less than one lost hire.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is there a completely free ATS with no limits?

No commercial ATS offers unlimited features at $0 forever. The closest options are Dover (free forever with unlimited jobs and AI features) and MightyRecruiter (unlimited jobs, users, and job board posting). Open source platforms like [Reqcore](/) have zero feature restrictions — you pay only for hosting infrastructure, typically $5–$20/month.

### What is the best free ATS for a startup with under 10 employees?

Dover is the strongest all-around choice: genuinely free, unlimited jobs, AI candidate sorting, and 75+ job board integrations. For tech startups hiring engineers specifically, Wellfound gives you access to a talent pool of startup-minded candidates that other platforms cannot match.

### Can I use a free ATS for GDPR compliance?

Free ATS plans almost never include consent management, data retention policies, or right-to-deletion workflows. If you process EU candidate data, you need either a paid plan with compliance features or a self-hosted ATS like [Reqcore](/) where you control data processing directly and can implement deletion workflows yourself.

### Should I use a free ATS or track candidates in a spreadsheet?

Use a free ATS from hire number one. Spreadsheets have no audit trail, no automated email, no collaboration structure, and no candidate pipeline visualization. Even Zoho Recruit's limited free plan (one active job) gives you more control and professionalism than a Google Sheet. The question is not whether to use an ATS — it is which free model fits your current stage.

## The Bottom Line

The best free ATS for your startup depends on what trade-offs you accept. If you want zero setup and AI-powered hiring, Dover is the clear answer. If you want to own your candidate data with no vendor dependency, [Reqcore](/) gives you the full software under an MIT license. If you need access to startup-native tech talent, Wellfound's free platform is unmatched.
